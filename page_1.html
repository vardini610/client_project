Study spaces

Shapiro Floor 2

More talkative study areas, especially in the evenings.

Location is: click here

Known by many as the “social” floor, this space is filled with long rectangular tables and wooden chairs mixed in with book stacks. 12 bookable study rooms border the floor, each containing a table, chairs, and chalkboard or whiteboard. Also on the floor is a soft seating area in the browsing collection and two computer labs. The noise level gets louder on this floor as it fills up for late-night study sessions.

Bert's Study Lounge

A café-like atmosphere with mixed seating, ideal for group meetings.

Location is: click here

This space near Bert’s Café has mixed seating that is ideal for group meetings. It’s also a great place to grab a snack and take a break if you’re on Central Campus. Screens mounted to the walls can hook up to your computer with an HDMI connection. Ask at the Shapiro Lobby desk for a cord and adapters. Find out how to display your own week-long digital showcase on the screens. Art on the wall was inspired by The Birds of America, the U-M Library’s first book.
